### High-resolution climate projections using machine learning reveals escalating future bleaching risk across the Great Barrier Reef

Ajitha Cyriac1*, Chaojiao Sun1, John Taylor2,3, Richard Matear4, Scott A. Condie4, P. Jyoteeshkumar Reddy4, Tongliang Liu5

1CSIRO Environment, IOMRC, Crawley, WA, Australia 2CSIRO Data61, Canberra, ACT, Australia 3Australian National University, Canberra, ACT, Australia 4CSIRO Environment, Hobart, TAS, Australia 5University of Sydney, Camperdown, NSW, Australia

*Corresponding author: Ajitha Cyriac (ajitha.cyriac@csiro.au)

Coral reefs are facing increasing threats of mass coral bleaching and extinction due to a warming climate. Extreme events, such as marine heat waves, are projected to become more frequent and severe in the future. In this study, we analyse various thermal stress metrics for the Great Barrier Reef (GBR) derived from the Coupled Model Intercomparison Project Phase 6 (CMIP6) projections. We downscale historic and future projections from five Global Climate Models, under different emission scenarios. The projections are downscaled to 10 km using a machine learning (ML) model based on super resolution deconvolutional neural network (SRDN). The ML model is trained on high-resolution model simulations of degree heating weeks (DHWs), a commonly used metric to analyse coral bleaching risks. Our results show that the intensity, duration and frequency of severe bleaching events (DHW > 8 degreeC-weeks) will continue to increase on the GBR under every emission scenario. By the end of the century, coral bleaching will be an annual event with a duration of more than seven months under the high emission scenario SSP5-8.5, leaving no time for corals to recover. By 2080, the central and southern GBR will experience higher overall thermal stress than the northern GBR, suggesting possible locations of climate refugia in the northern GBR.

#### Data availability

The OFAM3 data is available from the NCI data repository at https://doi.org/10.4225/41/5a77ab8dbbfe8. The CMIP6 data are publicly available from corresponding ESGF nodes: CNRM-ESM2-1 (Seferian, 2018), ACCESS-CM2 (Dix et al., 2020), ACCESS-ESM1-5(Ziehn et al., 2019), MRI-ESM2-0 (Yukimoto, Koshiro, et al., 2019), and NOAA GFDL-ESM4 (Krasting et al., 2018). The daily NOAA Coral Reef Watch data is publicly available at https://www.star.nesdis.noaa.gov/pub/socd/mecb/crw/data/5km/v3.1_op/nc/v1.0/daily/sst/.
